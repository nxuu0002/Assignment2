COVID-19 in Australia

COVID-19 becomes a poupular issue recently, because it is closely related to everyone's health and daily life. This visualisation is a summary of COVID-19 situation in Australia from January to October.

Positive Rate WorldWide

The stacked bar chart above shows the postive rate in Africa, Asia, Europe, North America, Oceania and South America. It is clear to see that South America has the highest positive rate among test results . 98% of the tests results appear to be positive. Besides, Oceania appears to have the lowest positive rate among the seven continents, the number of people who shows a positive is less than 1% of all people who participated in the test.

Case Count by State in Australia

The map above is a choropleth map shows count of case from January to October by states in Australia. The darker the color, the more people infected with COVID-19 in this state. From the chart, the most serve area is Victoria. Besides, Northern Territory has the least people of infected with COVID-19.

Case Count by Age Group and Gender in Australia

The grouped bar chart illustrates count of people infected with COVID-19 by gender in different age groups. Case count in age group 25-19 appear to have the highest number, in this age group, more female are infected than male. Age goups of 00-04, 05-09, 75-79 and 80-84 have similar infected population, which have relative low population infected compared with other age groups.

Case Change by State from January to October in Australia

The scatter plot above shows case change from January to October by states in Australia. The bigger the bubble, the more people infected with COVID-19 in the corresponding month. The filters below the scatterplot can be used to select a certain month or a certain state to better observe the data.

Cumulative Case Count In Victoria

Victoria is known to have the most people of infected with covid-19. This line chart shows cumulative case count in Victoria from January to October. From the chart, the number of people infected with COVID-19 increased rapidly from June to August.
